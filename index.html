CS1: Project Outline & Strategy

CHASE DYE
Fitness Modeling
Brand Launch
Digital Marketing

Project Focus

The Premise

About to graduate college with a digital marketing major, if I chose to walk away and pursue the career I actually want it would be: fitness modeling. This project documents the process of building that brand from zero: choosing the visual identity, developing the messaging, creating content, and establishing a presence across the platforms where fitness talent gets discovered and hired.

This is Scenario B, "rebranding from scratch". The brand is "me". The product is my presence in the fitness and athletic modeling space.

Project Overview

What This Project Builds

A complete content marketing strategy and personal brand built from the ground up, covering brand identity, audience development, platform strategy, visual content, video production, and written copy. Every deliverable is designed to position Chase Dye as a credible, bookable fitness model with a distinct look and a consistent online presence.

By the end of the semester, this portfolio will function like a real agency-level brand launch, one that can be shown to talent agencies, athletic brands, and sponsorship partners.

Goals & Measurement

Goal 1: Awareness

Build brand recognition by publishing consistent, high-quality content across fitness-relevant platforms and establishing a professional online presence that attracts industry attention.

Goal 2: Positioning

Establish Chase Dye as a credible fitness modeling brand through a strong visual identity, compelling storytelling, and content that showcases both physique and personality.

Measurement

Tracked through engagement metrics: views, impressions, likes, comments, shares, profile visits, and follower growth across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.

Quality Check

Consistency of branding across all touchpoints, quality of visual and written assets, and completion of the full planned content schedule.

CS4: Brand Story

BRANDING

Overall Story

What My Brand Is About

Fitness has been a big part of my life for years, so that's where the brand started. Once I got into modeling and content creation, it made sense to pull everything together instead of treating them like separate things.

I want to post about fitness, modeling, clothing, whatever's actually going on. Nothing too staged, nothing that doesn't feel like me. Some of it'll be planned out, but I also want to show the less polished stuff too.

The bigger idea is just getting better and being confident in what you're working toward. Fitness made me more disciplined and comfortable in my own skin, and that's the thing I want to come through in the content.

On top of that, I want the brand to open doors with fitness and travel companies. The goal is content that still feels like me but that a brand would actually want to be attached to.

Identity Brief

My Main Focus

Fitness modeling mixed with lifestyle content. I'd rather build a career around things I actually enjoy than force content that doesn't fit. Confidence, consistency, fitness, personal growth, basically all of those things kept real.

Personality

If My Brand Was a Person

01

Confident

Comfortable being seen. Doesn't need to act like the best person in the room.

02

Driven

Actually working toward something.

03

Social

Easy to talk to, gets along with different kinds of people.

04

Active

Always doing something gym, or outside, whatever's next.

Probably in fitted gym clothes, swimwear, or simple streetwear. Takes the goals seriously but still knows how to have fun. Has no patience for people who perform a personality they don't actually have.

Brand Voice

How I Want It to Sound

Confident but casual. Like I'm just talking, not reading off a script. I can still push people to work harder.

Direct, positive, easy to read. A little humor when it fits, because that's more natural for me anyway.

Example

“Wasn’t really feeling this workout at first, but we got it done and I feel even better now that it's over.”

That's the kind of thing I'd actually post.

Brand Values

What Matters to Me

Confidence

Being comfortable with yourself and what you bring to the table.

Consistency

Showing up even when the motivation isn't really there.

Growth

Getting better without pretending everything's already figured out.

Authenticity

Content that still feels like me, not a version of me that performs for the camera.

CS6: Content Draft

CREATE

Five Instagram Content Ideas

Five posts I put together for this assignment. Wanted them to actually feel different from each other.

Type of Content

Instagram photo posts and graphics

Software Used

Canva

Subject

Fitness, "confidence" and modeling

Audience

People interested in fitness and self-improvement content

Purpose

Build trust, and engagement

How It Fits the Strategy

Help people find the page and actually want to stick around

Post 1

You Don't Need a Perfect Workout

Sometimes just showing up is enough. For people who feel like they need everything figured out before they even start.

Subject: Workout motivation

Audience: People who are new to fitness or nervous about getting started

Purpose: Get people out of their heads and into the gym

How it moves people forward: If someone sees this and feels understood, they're more likely to follow for more of the same.

Post 2

More Than the Gym

Fitness is a big part of it, but I also want people to see the modeling, travel, and just everyday stuff.

Subject: Lifestyle and personal branding

Audience: People who follow fitness, lifestyle, and modeling content

Purpose: Show there's more to the page than gym content

How it moves people forward: Makes the page feel like a real person, not just a fitness account.

Post 3

Confidence Takes Practice

More reflective than the other posts. Confidence isn't something you just have it builds over time, and I think people relate to that.

Subject: Confidence and self-improvement

Audience: Young adults who are interested in mindset and personal growth

Purpose: Give people something real to connect with

How it moves people forward: Shows something beyond the physical. People connect with the mindset side as much as the fitness side.

Post 4

Behind the Scenes

Most people only see the finished product. This one pulls back the curtain a bit what actually goes into getting a shot.

Subject: Content creation process

Audience: Current followers and people interested in modeling or content creation

Purpose: Show the process, not just the result

How it moves people forward: People trust you more when they see how the sausage is made. Keeps current followers in the loop on what's coming.

Post 5

Small Wins Add Up

Progress usually isn't from one big moment. It's the small stuff done consistently. Learned that through fitness, but it applies everywhere.

Subject: Consistency and progress

Audience: Fitness beginners and people working toward personal goals

Purpose: Remind people that small, consistent effort actually adds up

How it moves people forward: Realistic and relatable. Makes people want to keep following for advice that doesn't feel out of reach.

How These Fit My Overall Strategy

These aren't random. Some introduce who I am, some show the process, some go a little deeper. If someone landed on my page for the first time and scrolled through these, they'd get a pretty good read on what the brand is about.

The first couple build awareness and get people connected to the message. The lifestyle and behind-the-scenes ones build trust because they show more personality. The end goal is turning new visitors into followers and making the page something brands and agencies actually want to work with.

CS7: Content Draft

PRODUCE

Five TikTok / Instagram Reels

Short-form vertical video content built around fitness, lifestyle, and personal brand. Each clip is shot to feel natural real gym footage, real moments.

Type of Content

TikTok videos / Instagram Reels

Software Used

CapCut, iPhone camera

Subject

Fitness training, gym lifestyle, and personal brand moments

Audience

Fitness enthusiasts, 18–34, who follow creators for workout motivation and lifestyle content

Purpose / Objective

Build awareness, showcase athleticism, and attract brand and agency attention

How It Drives the Funnel

Short-form video is the fastest path to discovery. Views turn into profile visits, profile visits turn into follows, follows turn into partnership opportunities.

Reel 1

Gym Session

Raw training footage showing the actual work. No voiceover, just the grind.

Subject: Workout / training content

Audience: Fitness enthusiasts looking for motivation and training inspo

Purpose: Show athleticism and consistency in the gym

How it moves people forward: People follow accounts that make them want to go train. This is that kind of content.

Reel 2

Lifestyle Clip

A look at the day-to-day outside of the gym. Fitness is a big part of the brand, but not the whole thing.

Subject: Lifestyle and personal brand

Audience: Followers interested in more than just workouts

Purpose: Show personality and range beyond fitness content

How it moves people forward: Brands want to partner with people, not just physiques. This content shows there's a real person behind the page.

Reel 3

Training Detail

Closer look at a specific movement or exercise. The kind of clip people save and come back to.

Subject: Workout technique and training focus

Audience: People actively working on their fitness who want practical content

Purpose: Provide value that keeps people coming back to the page

How it moves people forward: Saves and shares extend reach. Useful content builds trust faster than promotional content does.

Reel 4

Behind the Camera

The part most people don't see — what it actually takes to set up and shoot content.

Subject: Content creation process and behind-the-scenes

Audience: Aspiring creators and anyone curious about how content gets made

Purpose: Build authenticity by showing the work behind the work

How it moves people forward: Transparency builds connection. People trust creators who show the real process, not just the polished result.

Reel 5

Brand Moment

A clip that ties the whole identity together — fitness, confidence, and the overall Chase Dye look and feel.

Subject: Personal brand and identity

Audience: Potential followers, fitness brands, and talent scouts

Purpose: Leave a strong first impression that summarizes what the brand is about

How it moves people forward: First impressions drive follows and partnership inquiries. This is the clip that makes someone think, "I want to work with this person."

How These Fit My Overall Strategy

Short-form video is where fitness creators get discovered. These five reels cover the core pillars of the brand: actual training, lifestyle moments, technique content, the creation process, and the overall identity. Together they give a complete picture of who Chase Dye is.

The goal wasn't to make five of the same video. Each one serves a different purpose in the funnel — some pull in new viewers through algorithm discovery, some convert casual viewers into followers, and some show brand partners that this account is worth working with.

Posting this kind of content consistently is what turns a personal brand into something agencies and sponsors actually reach out to.
